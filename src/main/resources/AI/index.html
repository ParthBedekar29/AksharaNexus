How did Phoenician merchants finance long-distance trade?
Oracle · Phoenicia Phoenician merchants relied on a combination of family partnerships, profit-sharing ventures known as kārū-style contracts, and credit extended by temple institutions. Silver-weight standards allowed cross-regional exchange without coinage. Risk was spread by forming syndicates for individual voyages… Sources: Aubet (2001) · Markoe (2000) · Heltzer (1978)
Did they have any kind of insurance against shipwrecks?
Oracle · Phoenicia Direct evidence is limited, but bottomry loans — where a creditor bore the sea risk in exchange for higher interest — served a similar function. If the ship was lost, the loan was cancelled… Sources: Heltzer (1978) · Aubet (2001)
