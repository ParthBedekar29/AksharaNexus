About

What is AksharaNexus?

Historical knowledge is fragmented. It exists across papers, dissertations, institutional repositories, and wikis — with no consistent structure, no clear authorship, and no way to track how our understanding of a civilization has changed over time.

The fundamental problem with Wikipedia is not accuracy — it is accountability. There is no way to know whether a given passage was written by a domain expert or not.

AksharaNexus addresses this by restricting authorship to verified researchers and students affiliated with registered universities. Every piece of data is committed with a name, an institution, a timestamp, and a message — making the provenance of every claim traceable.

The structured data produced here will feed a central archive that is clean enough to power AI-assisted querying for end users — researchers, students, and the general public.

Process

How it works

Data on AksharaNexus follows a structured pipeline from institutional onboarding to the central archive.

  1. Institutions register and are verified

    Universities and research bodies apply for access. Once approved, an institutional admin account is created. Admins are responsible for managing their institution's presence on the platform.

  2. Admins create civilization trees and assign researchers

    Each civilization is represented as a hierarchical tree — a Root node with Volumes (broad periods or themes) and Entries (specific settlements, events, artifacts). Admins assign editor roles to verified researchers within their institution.

  3. Editors contribute attributed, versioned content

    Editors add and modify nodes in the tree. Every change produces a commit — a record containing the author's identity, their institution, a timestamp, a commit message, and a SHA-256 hash of the tree state. Any prior state can be restored.

  4. Validated data enters the central archive

    Contributions from all institutions are consolidated, deduplicated, and cleaned into a central dataset. This dataset will power a public-facing application allowing end users to query civilizational history with natural language.

Data model

How knowledge is structured

Every civilization on AksharaNexus is stored as a persistent tree. The structure is hierarchical, navigable, and designed to be queryable by both humans and AI systems.

Access model

Roles and permissions

Every person on AksharaNexus has a clearly defined role within their institution.

Role Who What they can do
Admin admin Institutional representative — department head, principal investigator, or faculty lead.
  • Create and manage civilization trees
  • Assign and revoke editor access
  • Rollback to any prior version
  • View all contributions from their institution
Editor editor Verified researcher or doctoral student assigned to specific civilization trees by their admin.
  • Add volumes and entries to assigned trees
  • Edit existing nodes with commit messages
  • View full version history of assigned trees
Viewer viewer Affiliated student or observer with read-only access to their institution's trees.
  • Browse civilization trees and entries
  • Explore version history
  • No write or administrative access
